Verbs

What is a verb?

Verbs are used in every context. It's the action made by the subject. You cannot write a complete sentence without using any verbs. Verbs are the only way in which a we can identify the tense of the sentence. Through tenses and verbs, we can stablish a coherent conversation and we can know if the person is talking in future, past or present. We also know that they need to go in a sentence and that they have to agree with the subject of a clause in English.

When should we use a verb?

In formal or academic English, you need to use one main verb phrase in every clause in English. This will come directly after the subject in most clauses, because English is what is called a 'subject-dominant' language. You can also use verbs before and after this main verb. For instance, in subject position or after the main verb, but these verbs have to be tense-less. What does that mean? It means you can use a participle form (for example: verb-ing or verb-ed) or an infinitive form but not something that could act as a main verb phrase.
